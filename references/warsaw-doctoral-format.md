# University of Warsaw / WNKS Doctoral Dissertation Format Notes

Use this reference for this user's University of Warsaw / Faculty of Culture and Arts doctoral dissertation formatting. It is a dissertation-track supplement, not a journal rule.

## Official Sources to Check

Before final submission, re-check current official UW/WNKS pages and local saved copies. The currently verified sources are:

- University of Warsaw Senate Resolution no. 157 of 29 June 2022 on doctoral and habilitation procedures: `https://monitor.uw.edu.pl/Lists/Uchway/Attachments/6245/M.2022.159.U.157.pdf`
- WNKS required-documents PDF: `https://wnks.uw.edu.pl/wp-content/uploads/2022/03/WYKAZ-DOKUMENTOW-WYMAGANYCH-PRZY-ZLOZENIU-PRACY-DOKTORSKIEJ.pdf`
- WNKS doctoral title-page template: `https://wnks.uw.edu.pl/wp-content/uploads/2020/12/wzor-strony-tytulowej.doc`

Keep a project-local audit under the dissertation workspace when these requirements are checked.

## Confirmed UW/WNKS Requirements

- The final procedure uses an electronic dissertation file, including a PDF in APD / repository workflows.
- WNKS documentation asks for printed dissertation copies, an electronic PDF in one file, and summary material.
- If the dissertation is written in a foreign language, the package must include a Polish summary/abstract; an English summary is also required by the checked WNKS guidance.
- APD metadata includes title, summary, and keywords in required language versions: the dissertation language, English when the dissertation is not in English, and Polish when the dissertation is not in Polish.
- The WNKS title page template uses: University of Warsaw, Faculty of Culture and Arts, author name, dissertation title, supervisor statement with academic title/degree and name, Warsaw and year.
- Public/repository steps are part of the doctoral package, so final files should be clean of comments, tracked changes, local path notes, and unfinished internal caveats.

## What Is Not Specified in the Checked Official Sources

The checked UW/WNKS documents do not provide detailed micro-typography for font, margins, body line spacing, footnote line spacing, paragraph indent, or citation style. Do not falsely present the defaults below as official UW law. Treat them as conservative working defaults unless the supervisor or doctoral office supplies a stricter template.

## Working Dissertation Formatting Defaults

Use these defaults for this user's editable dissertation DOCX drafts unless a later official/supervisor template overrides them:

- Page size: A4.
- Margins: 1 inch / 2.54 cm.
- Body: Times New Roman, 12 pt, justified, double-spaced.
- Footnotes: real Word footnotes, Times New Roman, 10 pt, justified, single-spaced.
- Bibliography/reference list: Times New Roman, 12 pt; single-spaced within entries, with consistent spacing between entries when needed.
- Non-English source titles in English dissertation chapters: English title first, followed by original-language title in parentheses.
- Headings: Times New Roman, clear hierarchy, not over-styled.
- Titles of books, journals, dissertations, and standalone works: real Word italics, never literal Markdown asterisks.
- Footnote callouts: immediately after the supported word, phrase, sentence, or clause; paragraph-final notes only when the same source group supports the whole paragraph.
- Final submission package: generate PDF deliberately from the clean DOCX only when final submission formatting and front matter are ready.

## Chapter-Level QA Checklist

- [ ] Body paragraphs are justified.
- [ ] Body paragraphs are double-spaced.
- [ ] Footnote paragraphs are justified.
- [ ] Footnote paragraphs are single-spaced unless a later official rule requires otherwise.
- [ ] Footnote references and footnote definitions match.
- [ ] No literal Markdown asterisks remain in body, footnotes, or bibliography.
- [ ] No final-facing second-hand-transfer labels remain for exhausted-primary cases.
- [ ] Non-English source titles use English title followed by original-language title in parentheses.
- [ ] Title page and multilingual abstracts are handled at final full-dissertation assembly, not inserted ad hoc into single chapter drafts unless requested.
- [ ] Render or preview QA confirms the DOCX opens and pages flow without blank pages, clipped text, or severe footnote overflow.
